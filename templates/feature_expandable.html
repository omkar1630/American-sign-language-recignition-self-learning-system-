Expandable

Expandable ASL Recognition Systems are designed with scalability in mind, ensuring that the system can grow and evolve to accommodate new gestures, more complex signs, and even sentence-level recognition. These systems are often built with a modular approach, allowing the addition of new features without overhauling the entire architecture

Here's a deeper dive into the expandable features and future scope of an ASL recognition system:


  • 1. Dynamic Gesture Support: What It Means: Dynamic gesture support means that the system can handle new gestures and recognize them in real-time as soon as they are defined or added to the system.

  • Phrase and Sentence-Level Detection: What It Means: In addition to detecting individual signs (letters or words), the system can process and recognize phrases and entire sentences in ASL.

  • Integration with Speech or Text Output: What It Means: This feature allows the system to translate detected gestures into audible speech or text
